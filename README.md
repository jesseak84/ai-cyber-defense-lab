# AI Cyber Defense Lab

## Purpose

This repository documents my training path toward becoming an AI-era cyber investigator and defender.

My primary focus areas are:

- Digital Forensics and Incident Response, or DFIR
- Cyber Threat Intelligence, or CTI
- Phishing and fraud investigation
- AI security and AI-enabled threat defense
- Practical security automation with Python
- Healthcare cybersecurity risk awareness

This is not a collection of random notes. It is a structured portfolio designed to show practical investigation, documentation, and defensive security skills.

## Career Direction

My goal is to build toward roles such as:

- Security Analyst
- SOC Analyst with incident response focus
- Incident Response Analyst
- DFIR Analyst
- Cyber Threat Intelligence Analyst
- Phishing Analyst
- Fraud / Threat Analyst
- Trust & Safety Analyst
- AI Security Analyst

The long-term goal is to become highly capable at protecting people and organizations from cyberattacks, scams, AI-amplified threats, and misuse of AI systems.

## Background

I come from a healthcare operations, risk adjustment, and chart retrieval coordination background. That experience involved vendors, regulated workflows, documentation, process gaps, sensitive data, and operational risk.

This lab is designed to turn that background into a cybersecurity advantage by building technical investigation skills, defensive reasoning, and portfolio-ready proof.

## Ground Rules

All work in this repository follows strict legal and ethical boundaries.

Security testing is limited to:

- My own systems
- Local virtual machines
- Intentionally vulnerable machines
- Capture-the-Flag labs
- Training platforms
- Clearly authorized environments

This repository does not contain instructions or activity intended for attacking real systems, stealing credentials, evading detection in real environments, deploying malware, or conducting unauthorized access.

See `rules-of-engagement.md` for the full rules.

## AI Use Standard

AI is used as a tutor, analyst assistant, drafting partner, and review tool.

The standard is:

**AI-assisted, human-verified.**

AI may help explain concepts, summarize logs, draft checklists, generate study questions, and propose detection ideas. Final conclusions must be checked, understood, and defensible by me.
